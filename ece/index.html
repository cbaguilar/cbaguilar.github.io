One of my main regrets in college was not selecting a more tangibly physical engineering degree as my major or minor field of study. I love computer science, but without interaction with the physical world is mostly just flowcharts and text. I now have the lofty goal of gaining the level of understanding of Electrical engineering roughly equivalent to an undergraduate student at the University of California. I will aim to track my learning here for my own accountability and to share my learning resources with other people.

I have choesen to skip the courses that I already took in my Computer Science curriculum. Following UCLA's curriculum as a model, I have already completed the following:

  • Single and Multivariable Calculus
  • Linear Algebra
  • Differential Equations
  • Physics 1-3
  • Statistics and Probability
  • Introduction to digital circuit design
This leaves me with the following subjects to self-study:
  • Circuit Theory 1-2
  • Systems and Signals
  • Digital Signal Processing
  • Electromagnetics for Engineering
  • About eight more core courrses and a design project
