Mechanical Engineering Corporations and Career Paths

Automotive Engineering

Automotive engineers are a subset of mechanical engineers. They are tasked with designing, developing, and maintaining the physical structures of vehicles. When developing modern cars, automotive engineers must work closely with electrical and materials engineers.

Currenly, Toyota USA is hiring Mechanical Engineering interns to work in their Toyota Racing Development office in Costa Mesa, California. Interns will be assigned to teams related to their field of study and get exposed to work including:

  • Aerodynamics
  • Chassis engineering
  • CNC programming
  • Engine design and development
  • Engine production
  • Manufacturing
  • Simulation

Toyota is seeking Mechanical Engineering students who are Juniors or Seniors with a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or above. They must have excellent written and verbal skills and knowledge of engineering software.

Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning Engineering (HVAC)

Whenever any building has some form of heating or cooling, a mechanical engineer was likely involved in the design. Almost every habitable structure employs some form of heating, cooling, or ventilation. As a result, the HVAC industry maintains a steady demand for skilled mechanical engineers.

The United States Government hires HVAC engineers to design, build, and maintain the countless facilities in its possesion, domestically and abroad. Government-related work is unique in the fact that candidates are often required to maintain a security clearence (and almost always need to be US Citizens).

Qualifications:

  • US Citizen
  • One year of experience as a Mechanical Engineer
  • Successful completion of a professional engineering degree
    • School must be ABET accredited

Robotics Engineering

Mechanical engineers are responsible for the development and maintenence of new and existing robotics systems. In addition to pure mechanical engineering skills, robotics engineers are often expected to have a strong background in writing, testing, and debugging code.

One particular robotics company is Flexiv robotics, a small company that is driving innovation in the robotics industry. Robotics engineers at Flexiv research and develop new hardware, collaborate with designers, conduct experiements, and document design details to send them to overseas manufacturing facilities.

Qualifications:

  • Advanced degree in mechanical engineering
  • Research skills
  • Hands-on building/soldering/debugging/prototyping experience
  • Familiarity with C/C++ and MATLAB/Python
  • Understanding of motor control

Solar energy

Mechanical engineering is crucial to the development of solar-powered infrastructure. Many solar arrays use moving parts that must be developed by mechanical engineers. In addition, the production of solar cells is automated. These autonomous systems must be designed and built by mechanical engineers. The rapid rise of solar energy in recent years means that solar-based engineering jobs are in high demand.

SpaceX hires mechanical engineers to develop their solar arrays. Engineers will be required to document production, develop robotics system, optimize labor, and collaborate with electrical engineers to ensure a quality product on time.

Qualifications:

  • US Citizen
  • BS in electrical, mechanical, aerospace, or manufacturing engineering.
  • One year of experience in development of mechanical or electrical hardware systems.
  • Ability to create and deliver presentations for design reviews
  • Experience in fast-paced, iterative design
  • Experience with 3D CAD software (NX/Unigraphics,Caita,Solidworks)
  • Basic competency with SQL databases
