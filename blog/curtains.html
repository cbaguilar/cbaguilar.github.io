I now live in New York City. I've enjoyed the city ever since I interned here for the first time nearly four years ago. Although I've had very clear ideations for my future as a programmer for no less than a dozen years, New York did not cross my mind until around the middle of college.

Currently I live in a two-bedroom apartment with my cousin, who has been living in the city for a couple of years now. We live on the third floor of what I suspect was once a fairly large townhouse that was chopped into several tenement units, then re-merged into apartments with at least one bathroom each. Below us lives the super and his family. They occasionally grill food on their terrace when the weather is nice (my floor has no comparable outdoor space). On the ground floor are two women with whom I've interacted once. The awkward angle of the stairs descending to the basement force you to crawl with at least three points of contact. In theory the lower level would be a nice communal place to hang while waiting for your laundry, but much of the space is taken by random items associated with the super's contracting business. At night you can feel the rumble of far-off trains trains gently shaking the building through the bones of the earth.

Our apartment is smaller than the places I've lived in Los Angeles, though the narrowness of the rooms provides a slightly labyrinthine experience that almost makes the floorplan feel more sprawling than my more open apartments in Westwood. Plus, the decorations chosen by my cousin and her past roommate make the individual corners and walls much more memorable.

Seeing the joy and life captured in these photos, playbills, and (very sickly) houseplants made my bare room (and every room I've lived in since high school) seem all the more like a prison cell. Between entering college and wrapping up my M.S. I've lived in no less than ten tiny rooms of minimal decoration.

Knowing that I would leave any apartment at the end of a school year or internship made me very hesitant to invest in permanent comforts like lamps or physical textbooks. As such, almost as soon as I got the keys to the N.Y.C apartment I successfully scoured Facebook marketplace for the corporeal version of my favorite PDFs and picked up a couple of nice lights of an industrial-chic variety off the sidewalk.
